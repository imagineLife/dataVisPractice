A Categorical Data Problem

The maximum values in the census data are not numerical. The maximum value is categorical, categorizing a family income above 250K$ as simply "250K+". Unlike the rest of the income values, which are numbers and can easily be compared to other numbers, the maximum is unclear. The maximum is interesting to me, and I think the maximum is important in this chart, so having a vague representation of the information looks misleading and unfortunately incomplete.
For the sake of this project, I used $251,000 as the numerical value in place of the categorically labeled "250K+".

A Brief Look At The Range

The range is at least $214,000, where the Maximum is '$250K+' and the Minimum is $36,156. Due to the maximum income value recorded at '250K+', the range of town family incomes in CT can not be calculated based on this Census data.
